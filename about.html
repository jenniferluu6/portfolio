Background

Two careers,
one through-line.

I came to urban research through construction. I managed large-scale building projects at Turner, Hensel Phelps, and Fairstead — on healthcare campuses, medical centers, and NYCHA affordable housing in New York.

"I wanted to work upstream, on the questions that shape decisions before anyone breaks ground."

Managing occupied affordable housing renovations at NYCHA put me inside the machinery of how housing decisions get made. I saw firsthand how much of what happens to a neighborhood is determined long before a contractor arrives — by policy, by investment patterns, by who gets to name what's desirable. That's what brought me to Penn.

At the Weitzman School of Design, I've built a practice at the intersection of spatial analysis, machine learning, and housing policy. My thesis research uses NLP and Self-Organizing Maps to read real estate listing language as evidence of neighborhood change.

I presented this work at the AAG Annual Meeting in San Francisco in March 2026. I also work as a Research Analyst at the Penn Institute for Urban Research under Dr. Eugenia Birch, and previously contributed to NASA-funded climate and transportation research at CAtRES under Dr. Megan Ryerson.

I hold dual US and Canadian citizenship and have lived in Blacksburg, Chicago, Los Angeles, New York, Washington D.C., and Philadelphia. I'm graduating in May 2026 and open to research, analytics, and policy roles.

What drives me

Research with a point of view.

01

Equity first

The neighborhoods that get studied least usually need attention most. My research is oriented toward displacement, disinvestment, and the language used to obscure both.

02

Methods that answer questions

I'm interested in spatial analytics as a tool for answering policy questions, not as an end in itself. The method should follow the question.

03

Implementation matters

My experience inconstruction taught me the gap between good policy and good outcomes is usually an implementation problem. I bring that lens to research.
